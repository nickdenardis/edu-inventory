College News and Events

  • Finalists for 2012 Sophie Kerr Prize Announced Washington College has named five finalists for the famous Sophie Kerr Prize, the largest undergraduate literary prize in the nation, this year valued at more than $58,000.
  • Miller Library to Close May 12 for Extensive Renovation Work Expected to Last Four Months The renovation will greatly enhance the library's spaces for study, teaching and research and will provide a more comfortable climate-controlled building for all patrons.
  • Senior Schelberg One of 20 in Nation Named to USA Today's All-USA College Academic Team Washington College senior James H. Schelberg has been named to the 2011 USA Today All-USA College Academic Team, an honor bestowed on only 20 undergraduates for their academic and community achievements.
  • Washington College English Professor Rich Gillin Is Recognized as One of America’s Very Best A just-released publication from The Princeton Review includes news that students and alumni of Washington College have known for decades—that Richard Gillin is one of the best professors in the country.
  • Top Ranked Men’s Lacrosse Hosts Centennial Championship, Faces McDaniel Friday Afternoon, May 4 This weekend, for the first time in eight years, the Shoremen will host the Centennial Conference Men’s Lacrosse Championship in Chestertown.
  • Smithsonian “American Pictures” Series Concludes with Author James McBride on James Brown , May 12 Memoirist, novelist, and musician James McBride will conclude the 2012 “American Pictures” series with a riff on a dynamic 1969 photograph of soul music legend James Brown performing at the Shrine Auditorium.
  • Tribeca Films Founder Jane Rosenthal to Speak at Washington College Commencement, May 20 enowned film producer Jane Rosenthal, co-founder of Tribeca Productions and the Tribeca Film Festival, will address the graduates at Washington College’s 229th Commencement.
