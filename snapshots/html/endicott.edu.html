The Van Loan School of Graduate and Professional Studies offers a variety of educational programs and services designed to provide adult learners with quality credit and non-credit courses. Students enroll in Master, Bachelor, and Associate degree and Certificate programs, attending full or part-time, on-site or on-line.
Van Loan School of Graduate & Professional Studies



Endicott College recognizes the importance of providing its graduates with intercultural competencies that will prepare them both personally and professionally for life in an interdependent global community.
The Office of Undergraduate International Programs serves as a resource for students who wish to study abroad, for international students who are studying at Endicott's main campus, and for the campus community.
Undergraduate International Studies




Center For The Arts



Situated on the Boston's historic North Shore, our facilities are spectacular and unique. With over 230 wooded acres overlooking the Atlantic Ocean, Endicott College offers a tranquil, relaxing and inspiring environment for your social, residential or corporate event. Our caring and professional staff will provide you with personal assistance to ensure the success of your event, and we are confident that we will surpass your expectations in every respect.
Event & Conference Services



There is no other Boston north shore standard like the Wylie Inn and Conference Center at Endicott College - Situated on a picturesque stretch of the Atlantic Coast on Boston's North Shore, the Wylie Inn and Conference Center at Endicott College offers newly designed, well-appointed accommodations; state of the art IACC certified conference center and an elegant social event venue, Tupper Manor. Located just 20 miles north of Boston, in the historic town of Beverly, the Wylie Inn and Conference Center is ideal for a weekend getaway, retreat or conference.
The Wylie Center

More Events...

  • Endicott College Showcases Performing Arts in: Spring Showcase

    At the culmination of each semester the Performing Arts Department presents performances in dance, chamber music, musical theater, drama, solo voice and instruments. Audiences can see the fruits of this work on Monday, May 7 at 5:00 p.m. in the Rose Performance Hall, Center for the Arts. This program is free and open to the public, but you can reserve tickets at www.endicott.edu/centerforthearts or by leaving a message at the box office at (978) 998-7700. 

  • Endicotts Brendan Cronin Discusses His New Autobiography

    Endicott’s own master chef and author, Brendan Cronin will discuss his new autobiographical release, Cheffin’ – from Potatoes to Caviar on Tuesday, May 8th, from 4:00 to 5:30 p.m. in the Little Theatre, Halle Library.  The event is free and open to the public.

  • Endicott College Celebrates 25th Presidential Anniversary of Dr. Richard E. Wylie

    To honor the career of President Richard E. Wylie and celebrate the impressive list of improvements and achievements of Endicott College under his 25-year tenure, the College will host a Gala Celebration on May 5.

  • Alun Williams: Distinguishing Marks

    Endicott’s Center for the Arts will be exhibiting the works of New York artist, Alun Williams, in a solo show titled Distinguishing Marks from now – May 24, 2012 at the Spencer Presentation Gallery, Center for the Arts.  The exhibition is free and open to the public.

  • Endicott College Featured in "The Princeton Review's Guide to 322 Green Colleges" Created in Partnership with U.S. Green Building Council

    "The Princeton Review's Guide to 322 Green Colleges” is the only free, comprehensive guidebook profiling institutions of higher education that demonstrate a notable commitment to sustainability in their academic offerings, campus infrastructure, activities and career preparation.
