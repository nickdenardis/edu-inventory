    Undergraduate Admissions

    The University of Georgia continues to be a national leader in the number of students each year who receive the very top national academic scholarships. Since 1996, UGA has produced seven Rhodes Scholars, plus 37 Goldwater, ten Truman, seven Udall, six Merage, five Gates Cambridge, four Marshall and three Mitchell Scholars. UGA has produced 21 Fulbright Scholars in the past two years.

    The Arts at UGA

    UGA's Performing and Visual Arts Complex provides some of the finest facilities in the South for teaching, research and performance in the fine arts. The Complex includes the Hodgson School of Music; the Performing Arts Center, which has a 1,100-seat auditorium for major concerts and musical performances; the Lamar Dodd School of Art, which opened in 2008 and includes studios, lecture halls, galleries, a media center and an eco-friendly "green roof;" and the Georgia Museum of Art with 9,000 square feet of exhibition space.

UGA President Adams announces plans to step down

University of Georgia President Michael F. Adams informed the campus today that he will step down as president at the conclusion of the next fiscal year. At the end of his 16 years in office on June 30, 2013, Adams will stand fifth in length of service among the university's 21 presidents, tied with founding president Abraham Baldwin.

Georgia Children’s Chorus to perform

The Georgia Children’s Chorus will perform May 6 at 7 p.m. at the Hugh Hodgson Performing Arts Center. A 100-voice children's chorus comprised of children in the Athens area ages 8 -18, the Georgia Children’s Chorus is in-residence at the UGA Hugh Hodgson School of Music.

UGA softball team plays Arkansas

The UGA Lady Dogs softball team will play Arkansas three times in two days. On May 5, a doubleheader will get underway at 2:30 p.m. in the Jack Turner Stadium, with the second game starting at 5 p.m. that day. And on May 6 starting at 1:30, a third game with the Arkansas team will get underway, also at Jack Turner Stadium.

Stadion Classic continues, supports scholarships
Play continues through May 6 at the UGA golf course for the Stadion Golf Tournament, a stop on the PGA Nationwide Tour. The university is serving as one of the major leaders of the tournament, and proceeds will support needs-based scholarships through the UGA Foundation.
