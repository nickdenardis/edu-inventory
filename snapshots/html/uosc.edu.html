Trinidad University: The University of the Southern Caribbean is located in Trinidad and Tobago, the southern most island in the Caribbean, making the University of the Southern Caribbean a Caribbean university that people from all over the caribbean (Anguilla, Antigua and Barbuda, Aruba, Bahamas, Barbados, British Virgin Islands, Cayman Islands, Colombia, Dominica, Grenada, Guadeloupe, Jamaica, Martinique, Montserrat, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Lucia, Saint Martin (France), Saint Vincent and the Grenadines and Trinidad and Tobago) can come and obtain degrees as well as their masters. A university is an institution of higher education and research, which grants academic degrees in a variety of subjects. A university provides both undergraduate education and postgraduate education. In Trinidad and Tobago there are 3 Universities, they are: The University of Trinidad and Tobago, The University of the West Indies at St. Augustine, and The University of the Southern Caribbean. University of the Southern Caribbean (USC) is fully recognized as a tertiary level educational institution by the Government of Trinidad & Tobago, through its accrediting body, the ACTT (Accrediting Council of Trinidad & Tobago). 

The University of the Southern Caribbean, Trinidad and Tobago, is an Accredited Seventh Day Adventist University, offering Bachelors and Masters Degrees. GATE approved courses are indicated by the sign (GA). The current presedent of the University is Dr. Trevor Gardner (one of three universities in trinidad and tobago)
