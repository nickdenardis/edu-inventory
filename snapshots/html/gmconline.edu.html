GMC-Fairburn: 1098T Questions
12/07/2011

1098-T FAQ

Get your 1098T quicker and securely!   Elect to receive your 1098T via email.  It is fast and easy, just click the link below.

http://register.gmc.cc.ga.us

1.     Click Log In.

2.     Enter User ID (Firstname.Lastname)

3.     Enter your Web Advisor password.

4.     Click Submit

5.     Click Students

6.     Click 1098 Electronic Consent (Financial Information Section).

 

When does Georgia Military College mail the 1098-T forms?

 

1098-T forms will be sent via U.S. Mail no later than January 31, 2012.

 

 

What is the 1098-T form?

The information on the 1098-T form is being provided to assist the student with filing for the education tax benefits/tax credit deductions, such as the Lifetime Learning Tax Credit, Hope Scholarship, Tax Credit, or the Above-the-Line Tax Deduction for Qualified Higher Education Expenses. See the IRS website for more information.

 

 

Why did I get a 1098-T form?

 

Georgia Military College is required to send a 1098-T form to any student that was enrolled and had qualified expenses billed during the previous calendar year.        

 

How do I view my 1098-T form?

 

To view your 1098-T form log you must first log into your WebAdvisor account which can be found here. Once you are logged in click on the "View my 1098-T Forms" and select the appropriate year of the form you are trying to view.   

 

Why didn’t I get a 1098-T form?

 

  There are several reasons why a 1098-T form may not be generated or received:

·         If you received more scholarships and/or grants than the amount of qualified tuition and fees billed.

·         If you didn’t provide us with your Social Security Number (SSN)

·         If your 1098-T for was sent to an incorrect address.

 

 

What are Qualified Charges?

 

Qualified charges include tuition and mandatory fees a student is required to pay in order to be enrolled at or attend Georgia Military College.  Charges and fees associated with room, board, student activities, athletics, insurance, books, equipment, transportation, and similar personal, living, or family expenses are not qualified.

 

Why is Box 1 Empty?

 

Schools can choose to either report qualified charges by payments received for qualified charges or by amounts billed. Georgia Military College’s Financial software reports 1098T information by amounts billed.  Because of this, Box 1 will always be empty. Box 2 will contain any qualified charges billed for calendar year 2011.

 

 

Why do charges for Room and Board and various other charges not appear on on my 1098-T?

 

Charges and fees associated with a room, board, student activities, athletics, insurance, books, equipment, transportation, and similar personal expenses are not qualified charges allowed by the IRS.

 

 I still do not understand my 1098T, what do I do?

 

Email 1098T@gmc.cc.ga.us and Georgia Military College will provide a response within 48 hours.   In the email you will want to provide as much detail as possible as to why you do not understand your 1098-T form.
