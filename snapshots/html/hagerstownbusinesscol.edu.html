Welcome to Hagerstown Campus

Career-Focused Programs

Congratulations on taking the first step toward a bright future. At Kaplan University, we understand the significance of a good education. We offer career-oriented bachelor's and associate's degrees and certificates in business, health sciences, information technology, and legal studies at our Hagerstown, Maryland campus. Enroll in Kaplan University and see how we build futures, one success story at a time.

9 out of 10 Kaplan University campus graduates are employed.*

Learn how you can earn a college degree and be on the move toward a satisfying, rewarding career. Kaplan University offers career-focused curriculum, education for the real world, instructors who work in the field, support for busy adults, blended online/offline learning, and career placement services.

Skills You Can Use

When you enroll in Kaplan University, you can expect to learn skills sought after in todays job market. Our practical curriculum complements our industry-specific externships to help ensure a smooth transition into your new career.

The Right Education

Choosing the right school is no easy task. That's why we offer flexible day and evening classes, a broad assortment of programs from which to choose, a convenient location, a variety of career services and, most importantly, personal attention. At KaplanUniversity you are more than just the number on your student ID. We take pride in our students, so our students can take pride in us.

*Kaplan University calculates internal employment placement rates to ensure that its students are succeeding beyond graduation. Those rates are calculated as the number of students who graduated between July 1, 2009, to June 30, 2010, and were placed by December 31, 2010, divided by the total number of students who graduated between July 1, 2009, to June 30, 2010, and were available for placement. Graduates available for placement are those graduates who did not have a placement waiver or were not otherwise unavailable for placement for reasons including health-related issues, pregnancy, continuing education, or military service.
