The University of Western States Doctor of Chiropractic degree program produces graduates who are chiropractic physicians and serve as primary care physicians. Chiropractic physicians view each patient as a whole being and provide effective patient care by incorporating lifestyle counseling, nutritional advice, exercise and rehabilitation, modern physical therapy techniques and hands-on manipulation and mobilization procedures. Doctors of chiropractic are widely recognized as experts in the treatment of musculoskeletal disorders.

The University of Western States Massage Therapy program is built on the premise that therapeutic massage has proven benefits to the overall health of the client. Massage therapists help clients manage symptoms of stress, pain, injury, and illness by applying various massage techniques to the soft tissues and muscular structures of the body.

The UWS program spotlights therapeutic and rehabilitative massage techniques, while providing students with extensive hands-on experience and incomparable educational resources. The curriculum focuses on human anatomy, kinesiology and therapeutic massage techniques allowing you to work in tandem with other health science students towards an integrated, well-rounded education.

Currently, UWS offers two Master of Science Programs;

M.S. in Exercise and Sports Science: Program Overview
M.S. in Nutrition and Functional Medicine: Program Introduction

The M.S. In Exercise and Sports Science program available to students currently enrolled in the Doctor of Chiropractic Degree program. It is a professional masters degree program designed to provide students with advanced-practice level training in the prevention, evaluation and management of injuries and disorders affecting athletes and others participating in sports activities.

The M.S. in Nutrition and Functional Medicine is offered in nearly all of its entirety on-line and represents a unique collaborative endeavor between an accredited university (UWS) and educational material licensed from the Institute for Functional Medicine (IFM), the organization which founded and has developed the key functional medicine concepts.

The University of Western States offers a full complement of undergraduate science coursework to fulfill the entrance requirements for the Doctor of Chiropractic program.

Many states require a bachelors degree to obtain licensure as a Chiropractic Physician. If you have yet to earn your undergraduate degree, UWS will award the Bachelor of Science in Human Biology to students who successfully complete the first five quarters of the chiropractic program. The design of the BS degree is modeled after the general education components of traditional liberal arts biology majors.

University of Western States Continuing Education programs provide educational offerings through seminars, certifications, and diplomate programs for health professionals. These programs meet the ongoing educational needs and licensing requirements of health professionals worldwide to keep them abreast of the latest information, enhance their existing skills, and advise them of changes in current professional thought to keep their practices relevant and their care exemplary. UWS provides continuing education through face-to-face seminars throughout the country as well as through distance education.
